In Week 1 of SECIP 2025, we were introduced to the foundations of social innovation and entrepreneurship. Our team, consisting of members from Malaysia, China, India, and Indonesia, decided to tackle the issue of "Selling Waste to Reduce Pollution". This decision was made after considering problems that affect multiple countries and could benefit from a circular economy approach.

I shared insights from my experience in Germany, where supermarkets incentivize recycling by refunding customers who return plastic bottles. This example shaped our early thinking about community-based waste solutions.

I contributed to framing our problem statement and participated actively during the empathy mapping and brainstorming sessions. I also helped prepare the slide deck and designed a simple survey for understanding restaurant waste disposal practices.
